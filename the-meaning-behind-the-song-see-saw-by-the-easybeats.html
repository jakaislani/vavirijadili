The Meaning Behind The Song: See Saw by The Easybeats

May 01, 2024
blog
TitleSee Saw
ArtistThe Easybeats
Writer/ComposerGeorge Young & Harry Vanda
AlbumVigil (1968)
Release DateJune 28, 1968
GenrePop
ProducerMike Vaughan & The Easybeats

When it comes to capturing the essence of carefree and lively fun, “See Saw” by The Easybeats is the perfect embodiment. Released in 1968 as part of their album “Vigil,” this upbeat pop song continues to be loved by fans decades later. Despite its simple lyrics and catchy melody, the song holds a deeper meaning that resonates with listeners.

From the very first verse, “Seesaw Margery Daw, Seesaw Margery Daw,” The Easybeats take us on a metaphorical trip back to childhood, where see-saws were a common playground attraction. In this song, the see-saw represents life itself, a constant back-and-forth motion that can be exhilarating, unpredictable, and full of ups and downs. The playfulness of the lyrics, such as “Well, I do the mashed potato, And I do the boogaloo,” invokes a sense of joyful abandon and freedom.

“And I’m shakin’ and a-rollin’, Anyway you want me to,” hints at the idea of surrendering oneself to life’s twists and turns, being open to new experiences, and embracing whatever comes your way. It reflects the band’s willingness to go with the flow and enjoy the journey, no matter where it takes them.

The chorus, with its playful repetition of “Seesaw Margery Daw,” emphasizes the carefree and unburdened nature of the song. It encourages listeners to let go of their worries, switch on their joie de vivre, and embrace each moment with enthusiasm, much like a child on a see-saw.

Personally, “See Saw” holds a special place in my heart. Every time I listen to its infectious rhythm and sing along with the lyrics, I’m transported back to my own carefree childhood days. I vividly recall the laughter, the exhilaration of playing on a see-saw, and the sheer joy of being in the moment.

The Easybeats ingeniously capture the nostalgia and innocence of youth, evoking a sense of nostalgia and a longing for simpler times. Their reminder to “get yourself together babe, And we’ll go rockin’ on” is an invitation to embrace the spontaneity and fun that life has to offer, regardless of age or circumstance.

With its release in the late 1960s, “See Saw” became an anthem for a generation that was experiencing societal and cultural shifts. It perfectly encapsulated the spirit of the time – a time of liberation, exploration, and breaking free from societal constraints.

It’s worth noting that “See Saw” was produced by Mike Vaughan & The Easybeats. The band’s intention to create a feel-good track shines through in their energetic performance and catchy melodies. The song’s success can be attributed to their talent for crafting infectious pop tunes that resonate with audiences.

As the final notes of “See Saw” fade away, one can’t help but feel a renewed sense of optimism and a desire to embrace life’s ups and downs with open arms. The timeless message of the song resonates not only with the era it was released in but also with audiences today. It serves as a reminder to let go, have fun, and appreciate the simple pleasures in life.
